THE CONFUSING PARTS

Five things almost everyone gets wrong

Straight from Vodafone Qatar's official tariff document (effective 16 Feb 2025).

01

Three balances, one strict order

If you have Data, Flex and Credit (Riyal) balance at the same time, Vodafone always spends them in this order: Data first, then Flex, then Credit. You can't choose.

02

Minutes and SMS are simple. Data isn't.

1 local minute = 1 Flex. 1 SMS, local or international, same price, = 1 Flex. But data and international calls cost a different number of Flex per unit — see the calculator below.

03

QR 10 and QR 20 don't carry forward

Every other Flex recharge, QR15 and up, lets you carry unused Flex into a new period if you recharge with Flex again before it expires. QR10 and QR20 Flex always expire, no carry forward.

04

Flex stops working the moment you leave Qatar

Flex is strictly local. The instant you're roaming abroad, your Flex balance can't be used, you'll need a roaming pack instead.

05

The dial code you use changes everything

Dial *777*PIN# and choose "Flex" to get Flex points. Dial *127*PIN# instead and you get the card's normal credit or data benefit, no Flex at all. Same card, two completely different outcomes.
